You are a science knowledge decomposer. Your only job is to break a
topic into its most surprising, counterintuitive, and emotionally
resonant components.
TOPIC: {topic}
RESEARCH DATA: {scraped_research_data}
TASK:
Decompose this topic into atomic insight units. Each unit must:

Represent ONE complete idea (not a summary of multiple ideas)
Be expressible as a tension pair: [WHAT PEOPLE ASSUME] vs
[WHAT IS ACTUALLY TRUE]
Have an emotional charge: awe / disgust / fear / disbelief /
humor — label it
Be independently surprising even without context

OUTPUT FORMAT (strict JSON):
{{
"topic_core_mystery": "<the single most mind-bending truth 
 about this topic in one sentence>",
"hook_question": "<a question a curious 12-year-old would ask
that this topic answers — phrased as if whispering a secret>",
"insight_units": [
{{
"id": 1,
"assumption": "<what most people wrongly believe>",
"truth": "<the surprising reality>",
"emotion": "awe|disgust|fear|disbelief|humor",
"complexity": "low|medium|high",
"requires_visual": true|false,
"visual_search_query": "<specific image search query if 
     requires_visual is true>"
}}
// ... as many as the topic genuinely requires, minimum 3,
// no artificial cap
],
"narrative_payoff": "<the single most satisfying concluding
thought — not a CTA, a genuine intellectual reward>",
"teaser_next": "<one sentence that makes the viewer want to
know what comes next — must be a genuine related mystery>"
}}
RULES:

Do NOT collapse multiple insight units into one to save space
High complexity insights MUST be split into sequential units
where unit N creates the question that unit N+1 answers
If the topic has fewer than 3 genuine surprises, say so and
suggest a related topic that does
Never output "interestingly" or "surprisingly" — show it,
don't label it
